The 25 Year Old Problem
While Agile software development emphasizes flexibility and fast iteration in code, traditional database management remains rigid, often requiring slow, manual, and structured changes. Databases, requiring high data integrity and complex schema migrations, typically conflict with Agile’s "fail fast" approach. 
Rigidity vs. Velocity: Agile teams aim for short development cycles, but databases require careful, often slower, structural changes to maintain data integrity.
Data Governance: Managing large datasets often necessitates strict, non-agile governance, making rapid iterations difficult.
The "Anti-Pattern": Traditional database administrators (DBAs) may operate outside the Scrum team, creating a bottleneck that contradicts Agile's cross-functional team principle.
Since 2001, agile made code fluid. The database never followed.
